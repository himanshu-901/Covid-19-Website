how to prevent

how to prevent virus

wash your place

Clean your place & objects prone to high touch – Some of the common places or objects such as doorknobs and tabletops that may be accessed by everyone should be cleaned to avoid the transfer of the virus

maintain distance

If you have to be around people, maintain a distance of 2 meters or 6 feet from others around you as much as possible. In general, any gathering of over ten people should be avoided or conducted virtually.

don't touch face

Avoid touching your nose, mouth, or eyes too much unless your hands are washed. It’s best to avoid any visitors at this point

wash your hand

Clean your hands thoroughly – Immediately after you cough or sneeze, and also at other times, wash your hands for at least 20 seconds with soap water or use an alcohol-based sanitiser. The sanitiser should contain at least 60-95% of alcohol.

use napkin

It’s important to maintain proper hygiene to avoid transmission of the virus. You shouldn’t reuse facemasks or gloves and dispose of them right away.

wear a mask

Whenever you're outside in a public place, you should always wear a facemask. Cover your mouth while coughing and sneezing – You should also cover your mouth with a tissue while coughing or sneezing.

things you should DO

wash your hand

Clean your hands thoroughly – Immediately after you cough or sneeze, and also at other times, wash your hands for at least 20 seconds with soap water or use an alcohol-based sanitiser. The sanitiser should contain at least 60-95% of alcohol.

always wear a mask

Whenever you're outside in a public place, you should always wear a facemask. Cover your mouth while coughing and sneezing – You should also cover your mouth with a tissue while coughing or sneezing.

stay home during fever

Don’t leave home unless to get medical care – Restrict your activities just to your home and avoid visiting work, school, or any other public areas. You should also try to avoid public transport as much as possible.

things you should NOT DO

avoid close contact with animals

The virus can spread from people to animals during close contact. People with suspected or confirmed COVID-19 should avoid contact with animals, including pets, livestock, and wildlife.

avoid close contact with peoples

Keep yourself away from other people , including any pets – It’s better to keep yourself restricted to a separate room and also use a separate bathroom, if possible.

avoid crowded area
